# Comprehensive List of Mathematical Groupings

Mathematical groupings are symbols used to organize expressions, clarify operations, and define order of precedence. Below is a categorized list of commonly used grouping symbols.

## 1. Parentheses and Brackets

- **Parentheses (Round Brackets)**: \( ( ) \)  
  - Used for order of operations, function arguments, and grouping expressions.

- **Square Brackets**: \( [ ] \)  
  - Used for intervals, matrices, and nested expressions.

- **Curly Braces (Braces)**: \( \{ \} \)  
  - Used for sets, sequences, and function definitions.

## 2. Absolute Value and Norms

- **Absolute Value**: \( |x| \)  
  - Represents the magnitude of a number or vector.

- **Vector Norms**: \( \| x \| \)  
  - Represents different norms of a vector.

## 3. Summation and Product Notation

- **Summation Grouping**: \( \sum_{i=1}^{n} (expression) \)  
  - Defines a sum over indexed values.

- **Product Notation**: \( \prod_{i=1}^{n} (expression) \)  
  - Represents repeated multiplication.

## 4. Interval Notation

- **Closed Interval**: \( [a, b] \)  
  - Includes endpoints \( a \) and \( b \).

- **Open Interval**: \( (a, b) \)  
  - Excludes endpoints \( a \) and \( b \).

- **Half-Open Intervals**: \( [a, b) \) or \( (a, b] \)  
  - Includes only one endpoint.

## 5. Matrices and Determinants

- **Matrix Notation**: \( \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix} \)  
  - Defines a matrix.

- **Determinant Notation**: \( \begin{vmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{vmatrix} \)  
  - Represents the determinant of a matrix.

## 6. Floor and Ceiling Functions

- **Floor Function**: \( \lfloor x \rfloor \)  
  - Rounds down to the nearest integer.

- **Ceiling Function**: \( \lceil x \rceil \)  
  - Rounds up to the nearest integer.

## 7. Set Theory Groupings

- **Set Notation**: \( \{ a, b, c \} \)  
  - Defines a collection of elements.

- **Element of a Set**: \( x \in A \)  
  - Indicates that \( x \) is an element of set \( A \).

## 8. Function and Logic Notation

- **Function Definition**: \( f: X \to Y \)  
  - Maps elements from domain \( X \) to range \( Y \).

- **Logical Grouping**: \( ( P \wedge Q ) \Rightarrow R \)  
  - Groups logical conditions.
